How We Got Here

I got a call from my childhood best friend in late January of my freshman year of college. His name was Finn, and he was studying abroad in Scotland.

He had just booked tickets to solo travel through Italy during a short break in February. I remember sitting there realizing the world suddenly felt accessible.

Within an hour, I was looking at flights.

Still, I could not shake the feeling that this was one of those moments you either say yes to, or think about for the rest of your life.

Two weeks later, we were standing in Italy.

We traveled through cities with no real plans, slept on trains, missed connections, and figured things out as we went.

Somewhere between the missed trains and midnight walks, something shifted.

The world was accessible. Curiosity was worth following. Ambition did not have to stay inside a classroom.

If you take a risk with your time, things do not fall apart. You figure it out.

That trip changed everything.

In February of 2023, we made a goal: visit 30 countries in 3 years.

In March of 2026, we completed it.

How We Do It

The philosophy behind the journey — travel light, move fast, stay curious.

We look for cheap flights, overnight trains, flexible routes, and experiences that feel real. It all begins with google flights, we will book the cheapest and most direct route, then we start plugging in locations to google maps, and use tiktok, instagram, and internet blogs to refine our locations and what we want to see. Next stop is budget, we spend less then $100 a day, and that is firm. Food is easy, we eat out of grocrey stores and cheap snacks, coutnries in Asia and Africa are always cheaper. We often find housing on Airbnb, our requirement is a room with at least a double bed and a door that locks, we dont mind sharing a bathroom. One time we stayed in an airbnb in Zurich, when we showed up, we learned we were staying in the hosts personal bedroom, and therefore he was sleeping on a couch. Other cheap travel memories include in the midle of Morocco when we stopped on the side of the road for snacks only to realize we had 12 dinars cash, for the entire day. We bought cheap chocolate cookies and prayed the city we were going to had an ATM or took Visa.

We always travel light. We always go cheap. Its the only way that we can see as much as we do

Some tips from over the years: Bring an eye mask to sleep on planes, trains and buses. Our fave travel snack is Starburst's and Snickers - it always hits. The song that always reminds us of adventures is Geronimo by Sheppard.

The goal was never to travel perfectly. It was to keep going.

About Us

Finn and I (Xander) met in the third grade, and we were lucky enough to be in class together. This was the first time I had ever seen Finn, because he was homeschooled previously. A moment that has defined our friendship in a somewhat ironic way was May of third grade, when Finn was the only one not invited to my tenth grade birthday party.

Fast forward to middle school: neither of us had any friends, and we both found ourselves playing video games in a teacher's classroom during lunch. I always wanted to hang out with Finn, so much so that I would text his mother to organize play dates. One time it went too far, when I got a call from Finn at night saying, “stop it dude, don't talk to my mom, I will hang out when I want.”

In high school, we went our separate ways — Finn went to private Catholic school and I attended the local public school. Finn would reach out periodically to grab lunch or catch up, and we would talk about life. Little did I know that he was considering coming back to school where I went for our senior year.

He did, and the rest would be what I would refer to as loyalty. We spent time together when we could that year, and would never stop talking about traveling the world sometime in the future. The amount of Instagram reels we would send back and forth was insane. Now today, we celebrate a friendship that spans across the world with memories everywhere in between. We have learned that maintaining a friendship is hard, but when you talk often and make an effort to see each other, it works out quite well.

Since that first adventure, we’ve traveled through more than 30 countries, learned to navigate unfamiliar places as a team, and turned every unexpected detour into a story worth sharing.
